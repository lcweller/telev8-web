## 1. Company summary

telev8 is a cloud-based TV-as-a-Service (TVaaS) platform purpose-built for commercial venues — hotels, apartment communities, senior living, and other out-of-home environments. It runs on inexpensive consumer televisions over a property's existing copper, coax, or fiber wiring, with auto-configuration handled remotely. It delivers a modern app-based viewing experience — full cable lineups, streaming, casting, DVR, catch-up TV, and a fully customizable HTML5 guest-room experience — through a closed-loop private CDN with edge caching. Server-side dynamic ad insertion turns what has historically been a CapEx-heavy cost center for property owners into a recurring revenue stream.

*Source: telev8 GRX Overview pp. 4–10, 14–16; telev8 Partner Overview slides 4–8.*

## The Core Reframe

The single most important strategic move in telev8's positioning is the structural reframe of in-room television:

> Television is no longer a recurring expense. It is portfolio-wide digital real estate — an owned media network that generates revenue, deepens loyalty, and modernizes the guest experience.

This is not a feature pitch. It is a conceptual unlock that changes how a property owner sees the asset they already own. Every other claim telev8 makes is in service of this reframe.

### The cost-center-to-revenue-engine arc

| Before telev8 | After telev8 |
| --- | --- |
| Recurring expense | Recurring revenue |
| Cost center | Revenue engine |
| Fragmented, distributor-controlled deployments | Centrally governed, owned media network |
| Commodity utility | Strategic asset |
| Operational burden | Brand differentiator |

## 2. Primary audience

**Property owners and operators who write the deployment check.**

Four named verticals, in priority order:

1. **Hospitality** — hotel owners, brand decision-makers, hospitality operators
2. **Multi-family / MDU** — owners and developers of apartment communities and condominiums (often through Interact as the deployment partner)
3. **Senior living** — community owners and operators
4. **Out-of-home** — vacation rentals, time-shares, hospitals, restaurants, and other multi-tenant venues

What they care about, in their own language:
- CapEx and OpEx reduction
- Resident, guest, and tenant experience as a competitive differentiator
- Simpler operations, fewer support tickets, fewer truck rolls
- New revenue streams (targeted ad share, premium tiers, bundled services)
- Modernization without the disruption of rewiring or replacing TVs
- A vendor that will still be around in five years

**Secondary audiences** (acknowledged, not addressed in primary landing-page copy):
- Channel partners and system integrators
- Investors and capital partners
- Press and industry analysts
- Strategic technology partners

The end-resident or end-guest is a beneficiary in the value chain, **not** a buying audience. We don't write copy at them.

*Source: CLAUDE.md "Primary audience" + "Secondary audiences"; reinforced by audience targeting in telev8 GRX Overview p. 5 and Interact MDU flyer.*

## Audience Tiers

telev8 communications serve two audience tiers, and the work changes depending on which tier we're addressing.

**Executive tier** (CFO, COO, Chief Brand Officer, hospitality C-suite, investors): primary audience for top-of-funnel marketing — website hero, investor materials, press releases, partnership decks. They care about portfolio strategy, scale economics, governance, monetization, and competitive moats. Voice register: bold, declarative, structurally argued, financially literate, outcome-led.

**Operational tier** (general managers, IT directors, regional ops, property owners at the asset level): audience for lower-funnel materials — sales sheets, deployment guides, FAQs. They care about install complexity, ongoing support, day-to-day reliability. Voice register: practical, specific, plain-English, problem/solution framed.

**Default rule:** when audience is unspecified, default to the executive tier. Operational materials should be requested explicitly. The brand wins by getting executive meetings; operational materials close the deals those meetings produce.

## 4. Core value propositions

Five blocks. Each leads with the property-owner benefit, supported by a concrete proof point pulled from the source materials.

### 1. Lower CapEx and OpEx than legacy commercial TV
No commercial televisions required. No rewiring required. telev8 runs on inexpensive consumer TVs over the property's existing copper, coax, or fiber wiring — with auto-configuration to the TV handled remotely.
*Proof: telev8 GRX Overview p. 4 ("operates via property Wi-Fi… no requirement for expensive commercial TVs or need to rewire"); GRX detailed overview p. 15 ("Does not require Pro:Idiom or serial MPI/MTI interface… No need for internal ATSC TV tuner — only requires television monitor and speakers").*

### 2. Turn TV from a cost center into a revenue stream
Server-side dynamic ad insertion (SSAI / DAI) delivers targeted advertising in lieu of legacy programmatic ads — an industry first for commercial multi-tenant venues. CPM multiplier for content partners. New revenue line for property brands.
*Proof: telev8 Partner Overview slide 6; GRX Overview p. 6 + p. 15 ("75+ channels with full licensing for 100% ad insertion (14 to 16 minutes per channel per hour)"); CLAUDE.md "Why telev8 is different."*
*Flag: the GRX detailed overview p. 15 has a line that reads "Does require PII (personal identifiable information)" in the SSAI spec block, which contradicts every other source (Partner Overview slide 6 and CLAUDE.md both say "non-PII user data"). We are treating this as a typo in the source deck and writing public copy as **non-PII** — please confirm.*

### 3. A modern viewing experience the legacy guide screen can't touch
Full cable programming, streaming apps, casting, DVR, 72-hour catch-up TV, trick-play, search, VOD — delivered through the telev8 LiveTV app, built in partnership with TiVo, on AndroidTV, Apple TV, Amazon FireTV, Samsung Tizen, and LG WebOS.
*Proof: telev8 Partner Overview slides 5–6; GRX Overview pp. 8–9, 15 (Rich Features: nDVR trick play, 72-hour catch-up, casting, search, VOD).*

### 4. Cloud-native delivery built for scale and uptime
End-to-end multi-tenant closed-loop system with central CMS. Established global CDN with a 50 Gbps private network reaching 450+ points of presence across the U.S. and globally. 99.9% uptime. Edge caching for WAN efficiency. HEVC/H.265 with native 4K support.
*Proof: GRX Overview p. 15 (Transport & Content); Partner Overview slide 7.*

### 5. Backed by 15+ years of development and a deep IP moat
Exclusive TiVo partnership for hospitality, healthcare, senior living, MDU, and multi-tenant out-of-home. First "hospitality-tier" enterprise license for Android TV (in partnership with UEI). 15+ years of platform development, 177+ patents licensed.
*Proof: GRX Overview pp. 5, 10, 14–16; Partner Overview slides 5, 8.*
*Flag: the figure "$400+M invested" appears in GRX Overview p. 15. This is plausibly publishable but is the kind of stat that benefits from confirmation. I am holding it out of v1 public copy and recommending it stay in investor materials only — please confirm.*

## 6. StoryBrand SB7 mapping

Built strictly per `docs/storybrand-framework.md`. Property owner is hero. telev8 is guide.

### Character (hero)
A property owner or operator running a hotel, apartment community, senior living facility, or other multi-tenant venue. **What they want, in seven words:** *"a modern in-room TV that pays for itself."*

### Problem
- **Villain:** the legacy commercial-TV stack — Pro:Idiom-locked TVs, on-prem headends, opaque distribution chains, and a remote-and-guide UX that hasn't changed in fifteen years.
- **External:** legacy commercial TV is expensive (commercial TVs, headends, Pro:Idiom licensing), requires rewiring, requires truck rolls, looks dated, and can't deliver streaming or casting the way guests and residents now expect.
- **Internal:** property owners feel stuck — locked into vendors that no longer serve them, falling behind on the modernization curve, embarrassed to put a 2005-era TV menu in front of a 2026 guest.
- **Philosophical:** property owners shouldn't have to choose between modernizing the in-room experience and protecting their margins. Commercial TV should compete on the same plane as the rest of modern technology, not three generations behind it.

### Guide
- **Empathy statement:** *"We know what it costs to keep a legacy commercial-TV system alive — the truck rolls, the rewires, the Pro:Idiom invoices, the guide screen you'd never want to look at yourself. Property owners deserve better."*
- **Authority (three proof points):**
  1. Built in exclusive partnership with TiVo for out-of-home and commercial multi-tenant markets, on 15+ years of platform development and 177+ patents licensed.
  2. First "hospitality-tier" Android TV enterprise license (with Universal Electronics) — an industry first.
  3. Closed-loop private CDN with 450+ points of presence and 99.9% uptime.

### Plan (3 steps — process plan)
1. **Schedule a demo.** We walk you through the platform and audit your property's existing wiring, TVs, and PMS.
2. **Switch the stack.** telev8 provisions over your existing infrastructure — no rewire, no commercial TVs, no on-site headend.
3. **Watch the math change.** Lower CapEx and OpEx, a modern viewer experience, and a new ad-revenue line on day one.

### Plan (agreement plan — 3 promises)
- No commercial TVs required.
- No proprietary networks required.
- No PII collected from your guests or residents.

### Direct CTA
**"Schedule a demo"** — locked wording, used identically across the page. Matches plan step one per SB7.

### Transitional CTA
**"Download the platform overview"** — gated email capture in v1. The form returns a PDF/interactive overview (legacy-vs-telev8 distribution diagram + value-prop scoreboard) and the email lands in the marketing list.

### Failure (3–4 things to avoid)
- Continuing to spend on legacy commercial TV — Pro:Idiom licensing, headend hardware, commercial TV upgrades, truck rolls.
- Falling behind competitor properties that have already modernized.
- Missing the dynamic-ad-insertion revenue window while it's still differentiating.
- Watching guests and residents quietly cast around your in-room product instead of using it.

### Success (after-picture)
A property where every room runs a modern, app-based TV experience guests and residents actually use. CapEx down. OpEx down. A new monthly line of ad revenue. Truck rolls eliminated. A property that looks — and operates — like it's running 2026 technology, not 2005.

### Character transformation
**From:** "the operator who's been told modern TV isn't possible without ripping out the building."
**To:** "the operator who modernized the in-room experience and made it pay for itself."

## 7. Voice and tone

Anchored in `CLAUDE.md` and tested against the existing source materials.

### Default posture
- **Confident without bravado.** State capabilities directly. No "we believe," "we strive to," "we aim."
- **Contrast-driven.** The strongest telev8 stories are framed against the legacy. Use that contrast deliberately.
- **Plain-spoken value, technical authority underneath.** Lead with the property-owner benefit. Technical proof serves as evidence, not the headline.
- **Challenger energy.** telev8 is challenging cable, satellite, and commercial-TV incumbents. Sound like it.
- **lowercase telev8.** Always — never "Telev8" or "TELEV8" except in legal contexts.

### Tone calibration
| Axis | Where telev8 lands |
| --- | --- |
| Formal ↔ Conversational | Conversational-but-credible |
| Technical ↔ Plain-English | Plain-English first; technical depth on demand |
| Measured ↔ Bold | Bold and declarative |
| Visionary ↔ Pragmatic | Pragmatic with visionary moments |
| Serious ↔ Playful | Mostly serious, occasional dry wit |

### Voice exemplars

**Strong (preserve this register):**
> "cut the cost. not the cord."
> "transforming commercial television."
> "streaming television, reimagined."
> "a user experience reimagined."
> "we don't sell hardware. we turn the tv in every room into the most flexible asset on your property."

**Weak (do not produce):**
> "telev8 is a leading provider of next-generation, AI-powered television solutions designed to seamlessly transform the modern hospitality experience."
> "Our world-class team is committed to delivering innovative, cutting-edge technology that empowers our valued partners."

## 8. Words and phrases to favor

- **Contrast scaffolding:** "while legacy systems X, telev8 Y"
- **(r)evolution** — the parenthetical-r device is established brand equity. Use sparingly.
- **cut the cost — not the cord.** — preserve as a hero line; use thoughtfully, never as filler.
- **transforming commercial television.** — secondary anchor phrase.
- **industry first** — only when earned and provable.
- **reimagined**, **reshaped**, **modernized** — used selectively so they retain weight.
- **closed-loop**, **edge cache**, **app-based**, **cloud-native** — technical proof words used at the layer below the headline.
- **property owners**, **operators**, **brands** — name the buyer, not the building.
- Concrete numbers and proof points (177+ patents, 450+ POPs, 99.9% uptime, 75+ channels, 15+ years).
- Active voice. Short, declarative sentences punctuated by longer rhythmic ones.

### Conceptual frames (executive register)

- **digital real estate** — the in-room TV as a property the operator owns. Property-owner language.
- **owned media network** — positions telev8 as marketing infrastructure, not a vendor. CMO language.
- **revenue engine** — what telev8 turns the TV into.
- **portfolio-wide** — the modifier of choice for scale claims (vs. weaker alternatives like "across all your properties" or "company-wide").
- **at scale** — used to set up financial or operational projections.
- **strategic asset** / **strategic partner** / **strategic placement** — qualifier for high-value items.
- **critical mass** — used when the argument depends on adoption velocity.

### Verbs of intentionality

These signal that telev8 was deliberately built — not assembled, not pieced together. Always point to a specific design choice.

- **purpose-built** / **purpose-designed**
- **engineered for**
- **architected to**
- **designed to**
- **built for**

### Transformation language

Used sparingly so the words retain weight.

- **reimagines** — when reframing an existing concept.
- **reframes** — when shifting categorization (e.g., from cost center to revenue engine).
- **transforms** — earned when telev8 genuinely changes the structure of a property's economics.
- **modernizes** — earned when replacing legacy infrastructure.
- **unlocks** — earned when telev8 enables something previously impossible.

### Pain-point phrases (the "before" picture)

- **fragmented, distributor-controlled deployments**
- **locked-down hardware**
- **costly proprietary networks** that disrupt operations
- **disruptive renovations** and network overhauls
- **the truck rolls and license invoices**
- **operational burden**

## 9. Words and phrases to avoid

- **AI-powered.** telev8 is not an AI company.
- **Revolutionary**, **disrupt**, **disruptor.** Don't claim what isn't earned.
- **Next-generation.** Overused; describe what's actually new instead.
- **Best-in-class**, **world-class**, **cutting-edge**, **state-of-the-art.** Wallpaper.
- **Synergy**, **leverage**, **streamline.** Corporate filler.
- **Seamless.** Over-deployed; use only when literal.
- **Solutions.** As a vague catch-all. Use only when specifically the right word.
- **Hedging:** "we believe," "we strive to," "we aim to," "we are committed to."
- **Greenwashing or hand-waving** of any kind.
- **"Telev8" / "TELEV8"** in body or display copy.
- **Generic empathy** ("we care about our customers," "valued partners," "trusted partner").

## 10. Tagline candidates

The existing campaign line **"cut the cost — not the cord."** is brand equity. Preserve it. Below are candidate complementary lines — for hero, sub-hero, secondary positioning, or campaign use.

**Recommended hero (preserve):**
1. **cut the cost. not the cord.** — established, earned, and uniquely on-brand. *Use case:* primary tagline, hero subhead, footer signoff.

**New candidates (in the same register, ranked by my preference):**

2. **consumer tvs. commercial outcomes.** — the cleanest distillation of telev8's hardest-to-replicate differentiator: the same TV that's in every guest bedroom and every break room runs a commercial-grade platform. Hard contrast, two beats, lowercase. *Use case:* secondary tagline / hero kicker.

3. **stop maintaining tv. start monetizing it.** — flips the operator's existing posture toward TV and points directly at the DAI revenue thesis. Strong on stakes (the cost of doing nothing). *Use case:* mid-page section header or campaign line for the ad-revenue narrative.

4. **the (r)evolution runs on the wires you already have.** — preserves the "(r)evolution" brand device and connects it to the no-rewire promise — a story the owner cares about because it's their problem, not telev8's cleverness. *Use case:* hero subhead or longform section opener.

5. **every room. modernized. accountable.** — three beats; says the deployment is comprehensive (every room), the experience is current (modernized), and it pays for itself (accountable). *Use case:* secondary tagline; works inside a value-prop block.

I recommend leading with **#1 as preserved campaign line** plus **#2 as a fresh hero kicker** for the website specifically. Together they say *"every room runs a commercial-grade platform that costs less than what you have today."*

**LOCKED (user-approved):** Hero pair = **"cut the cost. not the cord."** (campaign line / footer) + **"consumer tvs. commercial outcomes."** (hero kicker). #3–#5 retained as a campaign-line library for future use.
